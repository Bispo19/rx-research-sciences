Peptide Storage & Handling Guide

Proper storage techniques are essential for maintaining peptide stability and ensuring reliable research results. Follow our comprehensive guide for optimal preservation.

View Storage Guidelines

Comprehensive Storage Guidelines

Follow these protocols to maintain peptide integrity and maximize shelf life

Temperature Storage

  • Optimal: -20°C or below for long-term storage
  • Short-term: 2-8°C for up to 30 days
  • Maximum: Never exceed 25°C
  • Freezer: Use frost-free freezers to prevent freeze-thaw cycles
-20°C Recommended

Environmental Protection

  • Moisture: Store in desiccated environment
  • Light: Protect from direct light exposure
  • Oxygen: Keep in airtight containers
  • Containers: Use amber vials for light-sensitive peptides
Desiccated & Dark

Shelf Life & Stability

  • 24+ months: At -20°C in sealed container
  • 12 months: At 2-8°C in sealed container
  • 1 month: At room temperature (15-25°C)
  • Check: Visual inspection before use
24+ Months Stability

Refrigerated Storage

  • Temperature: 2-8°C immediately after reconstitution
  • Duration: 30-60 days maximum
  • Container: Use original vial or sterile container
  • Labeling: Clearly mark date of reconstitution
2-8°C • 60 Days Max

Frozen Storage

  • Temperature: -20°C for extended storage
  • Duration: Up to 90 days when frozen
  • Aliquots: Divide into single-use aliquots
  • Thawing: Thaw slowly at 2-8°C
-20°C • 90 Days Max

Critical Warnings

  • DO NOT: Re-freeze after thawing
  • Avoid: Repeated freeze-thaw cycles
  • Discard: If cloudiness or precipitation occurs
  • Never: Store at room temperature
Critical Protocols

Shipping Protocols

  • Temperature: Shipped with ice packs at 2-8°C
  • Packaging: Insulated containers with temperature indicators
  • Duration: Expedited shipping (2-3 days)
  • Tracking: Real-time temperature monitoring available
Temperature Controlled

Receiving & Inspection

  • Inspect: Check temperature indicator upon arrival
  • Transfer: Move to storage immediately (within 30 minutes)
  • Document: Record receipt date and condition
  • Verify: Check vial integrity and labeling
Immediate Transfer

Handling Precautions

  • Gloves: Always wear nitrile gloves
  • Aseptic: Use sterile techniques when handling
  • Contamination: Avoid contact with skin or surfaces
  • Documentation: Maintain handling logs
Sterile Handling Required

Reconstitution Protocol

  • Water: Use bacteriostatic water only
  • Sterility: Wipe vial tops with alcohol swab
  • Technique: Use aseptic techniques throughout
  • Mixing: Gently swirl, do not shake vigorously
Aseptic Technique

Quality Control

  • Visual: Check for clarity before use
  • Labeling: Clearly mark all containers
  • Documentation: Maintain storage logs
  • Expiration: Follow FIFO (first in, first out)
Quality Assurance

Record Keeping

  • Dates: Record receipt, reconstitution, and expiry
  • Temperature: Log storage temperature daily
  • Usage: Document all aliquots and usage
  • Incidents: Record any deviations or issues
Complete Documentation

Peptide Stability Timeline

Understanding peptide degradation over time under different storage conditions

Day 0: Receipt

Immediate Action: Transfer to -20°C storage within 30 minutes of receipt. Inspect packaging and temperature indicators.

Day 1-730: Lyophilized Storage

Stability: Maintain at -20°C. Peptides remain ≥95% pure for up to 24 months when stored properly in sealed containers.

Day of Reconstitution

Critical Step: Use bacteriostatic water and aseptic technique. Label vial with reconstitution date. Store at 2-8°C immediately.

Day 1-30: Refrigerated Use

Optimal Period: Reconstituted peptides remain stable for 30 days at 2-8°C. Visual inspection required before each use.

Day 31-60: Extended Refrigeration

Caution: Some peptides may remain stable up to 60 days, but efficacy may decrease. Not recommended for critical research.

After 60 Days: Disposal

Required Action: Dispose of reconstituted peptides according to laboratory safety protocols. Never use beyond recommended period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about peptide storage and handling

Can I use sterile water instead of bacteriostatic water?

No. Bacteriostatic water contains antimicrobial agents that prevent bacterial growth. Sterile water lacks these preservatives and can lead to contamination within 24 hours. Always use bacteriostatic water for peptide reconstitution.

How many times can I freeze-thaw reconstituted peptides?

Zero times recommended. Freeze-thaw cycles degrade peptide structure. Aliquot reconstituted peptides into single-use portions to avoid repeated freezing and thawing.

What temperature indicator should I see upon receipt?

Our shipments include temperature indicators that should show "OK" or green color. If indicator shows "EXPOSED" or red, contact support immediately and do not use the peptides until verified.

Can I store peptides at -80°C instead of -20°C?

Yes, -80°C is excellent for long-term storage and can extend shelf life beyond 24 months. However, ensure proper packaging to prevent freeze-drying at ultra-low temperatures.

What if my peptide vial has condensation inside?

Condensation indicates moisture exposure. Lyophilized peptides are hygroscopic. If significant condensation is present, the peptide may have degraded. Contact support for replacement.

How should I dispose of expired peptides?

Follow your institution's chemical waste disposal protocols. Typically, peptides should be treated as biological/chemical waste and disposed through approved hazardous waste channels.
